About TaGSense

Study
An Analysis on Multilingual Transformers for Low-Resource Taglish Sentiment Analysis: Effects of Code-Switch Density
Institution Partido State University
Program BSCS 2026
Team Beldad · Burac · Cea · Guevara
Models
mBERT
Multilingual BERT by Google. Pre-trained on 104 languages using a shared WordPiece vocabulary. Fine-tuned with QLoRA adapters on the TAGCOL dataset.
XLM-RoBERTa
Cross-lingual RoBERTa by Facebook AI. Trained on 2.5TB of filtered CommonCrawl data across 100 languages. Known for strong performance on low-resource language tasks.
TLUnified-RoBERTa
RoBERTa pre-trained specifically on TLUnified — a large Filipino text corpus. Best suited for Tagalog-dominant and Taglish text.
Code-Switch Density

CSD measures the proportion of English tokens in a Taglish post, computed at the token level using a curated lexicon from the TAGCOL dataset.

CSD = English Tokens / Total Tokens

Objectives
  1. Construct a dataset of ~6,000–7,000 social media posts (Facebook, Reddit, YouTube) labelled positive (33%), negative (33%), or neutral (34%), annotated for emoji usage and token-level code-switching.
  2. Assess mBERT, XLM-RoBERTa, and TLUnified-RoBERTa on accuracy, macro-F1, and confusion matrices; fine-tune the best performer to competitive benchmarks against prior Taglish sentiment studies.
  3. Investigate whether curriculum learning improves macro-F1 by ≥ 5% across all five CSD bands; examine the effect of emoji annotation on classification accuracy.
  4. Identify failure modes — sarcasm, netspeak, slang, and binaliktad na salita — via qualitative error analysis and confusion matrices.
  5. Release the curated dataset and trained model as open resources for future Taglish NLP research.
Training Process

All three models share the same 9-phase pipeline run on Google Colab Free Tier (T4 GPU, 16 GB VRAM), combining QLoRA parameter-efficient fine-tuning and Density-Primary Self-Paced Curriculum Learning (SPCL).

Phase 0
Environment Setup
Google Drive is mounted for persistent storage across Colab disconnects. All dependencies (Transformers, PEFT, bitsandbytes, datasets, deep-translator, scikit-learn) are pinned for reproducibility. GPU availability is verified and a global DEVICE variable is set.
Phase 1
Dataset Loading & Band Splitting
The TAGCOL JSONL dataset is loaded with sentiment labels, CSD scores, emoji flags, and band assignments. Data is stratified 80/10/10 into train, validation, and test. Balanced class weights are computed and passed to a custom WeightedTrainer.
Phase 2
Warm-Up Pass & SPCL Scoring
A 2-epoch FP16 warm-up (no QLoRA) on 20% of training data produces a confidence score per sample — the max softmax probability for the predicted class. High = easy; low = hard/noisy. These scores drive within-band ordering in Phase 3.
Phase 3
Curriculum Ordering
Samples are arranged using Density-Primary, Confidence-Within-Band across four progressive stages — Tagalog-dominant easy samples first, up to English-dominant ambiguous ones last.
Stage 1Band 1 (all) + Band 2 (easy 50%) — Tagalog-dominant
Stage 2Band 2 (hard 50%) + Band 3 (easy 50%) — Transitional
Stage 3Band 3 (hard 50%) + Band 4 (all) — English-leaning
Stage 4Band 5 (all) — English-dominant, most ambiguous
Phase 4
QLoRA Configuration
Models load in 4-bit NF4 via bitsandbytes (~3 GB vs ~12 GB full FP32). LoRA adapters (r=16, alpha=32) are injected into all query/value projections. Frozen base weights preserve multilingual representations and reduce catastrophic forgetting.
Phase 5
Curriculum Fine-Tuning Loop
Each model trains through all four stages sequentially. LR decays (3×10⁻⁴ → 5×10⁻⁵) and epoch counts decrease (3→3→3→2) per stage to prevent overfitting on smaller later bands. Effective batch size 32 via gradient accumulation (8×4).
Phase 6
Baseline Fine-Tuning
Two baselines per model: a standard FP16 run (shuffled, 5 epochs, LR=2×10⁻⁵) and a QLoRA-only run (same setup as curriculum but shuffled) — isolating the curriculum ordering effect from QLoRA regularization.
Phase 7
Per-Band & Emoji Evaluation
Macro-F1 and confusion matrices are reported per CSD band. A second slice measures performance by emoji count — none, 1–3, and 4+ — to test whether emoji annotation improves or hurts accuracy.
Phase 8
Qualitative Failure Analysis
Misclassified samples are tagged by failure type: sarcasm/irony, binaliktad na salita, netspeak, and emoji-heavy. The resulting CSV is exported to Drive for Chapter 4.
Phase 9
Results Summary
All results aggregate into results_summary.csv — overall macro-F1, per-band macro-F1, and all three training conditions side by side. Confusion matrix plots and emoji JSONs are saved to Drive for thesis use.
